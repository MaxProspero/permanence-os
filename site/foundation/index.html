Common Questions

What is Ophtxn?

Ophtxn is a governed personal intelligence operating system. It wraps AI models with persistent memory, autonomous research agents, multi-channel communications, and human-in-the-loop approval gates. It is not a chatbot — it is a runtime that compounds context and decisions across sessions.

How is this different from ChatGPT or Claude?

ChatGPT and Claude are stateless conversational models — every session starts fresh. Ophtxn is an OS layer: it maintains memory across sessions, runs autonomous multi-agent workflows, enforces spend caps and approval queues, and gives you audit trails for every action. You keep control; the agents do the work.

What is a governed personal intelligence OS?

A category pioneered by Ophtxn. Agents plan and act autonomously, but irreversible or costly actions are queued for human approval rather than executed immediately. Every decision is logged with causal context — why the agent proposed it and what it expected to happen. Governance is architecture, not policy.

Is it free? What does it cost to run?

Ophtxn runs in no-spend local mode by default, using free or locally-hosted models. Paid API usage (Claude, GPT-4, etc.) is opt-in with configurable hard spend caps. The core runtime is open source at github.com/MaxProspero/permanence-os.

Who is it built for?

Founders, operators, and solo builders running high-volume execution stacks — people who need the leverage of autonomous AI without losing control over decisions, budget, or data. If you currently manage AI through 10 different browser tabs, Ophtxn is the single runtime that replaces them.
